Snowboard


This is the main piece of equipment you need to start snowboarding. Snowboards come in a wide variety of shapes, sizes, and materials meant for different aspects of snowboarding. For example, a stiff flex snowboard will move faster through terrain than a soft flex snowboard. A true-twin shaped snowboard better for tricks than a directional snowboard because the length and shape of the board is symmetrical. A directional snowboard is better for deep powder because setting back your stance helps keep the nose above the snow.

Boots


Your boots are the most important piece of your setup when it comes to comfort. If your boots don't fit or aren't comfortable, then you will not be able to ride properly. There are different lacing methods that help tighten your boots to ensure they fit and respond properly to your movements. Traditional laces that have to be tied like shoelaces get loose quickly especially when the laces get wet. A new lacing method called speed laces allow you to just pull upwards to tighten the laces. Another more intricate system is called the BOA lacing system. These have knobs that click in increments to tighten your boot to your liking. Some boots have two BOA lacing systems to tighten the area around your shins and instep separately.

Bindings


Bindings are an important part of your overall snowboard setup. There are different binding technologies that are meant to excel at different aspects of the sport. Like snowboards, bindings have a flex rating that affects how responsive they are to your movements. When bindings are more loose and playful, they allow for more tweaks in your tricks thus making the trick more stylish. Your snowboarding stance is also customized through your bindings using the baseplate underneath the footbed of the binding. A normal stance which is also known as "reference stance" is -15/+15 on the baseplate measurement.
