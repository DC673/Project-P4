50-50


The 50-50 is a standard beginner trick and it will likely be the first trick you learn when getting onto a features. Because you are already used to riding with the nose of your snowboard pointed in the direction you are moving, a 50-50 should feel the most natural when riding on a feature. The trick is called a 50-50 because it refers to the base of the board being half on and half off of a rail.

Boardslide


The boardslide is a classic trick that is a staple trick to build up to more advanced tricks and features. To do a boardslide, you need to turn your board 90 degrees so it's perpendicular to the feature you're riding on like the above picture. In this example, the rider is facing forward while sliding. This boardslide would be described as a backside boardslide because a feature would typically be behind you before getting onto it when doing a backside boardslide.

Tail Press


The tail press is a stylish trick with varying difficulty depending on the feature you're doing the trick on. On a wider feature like a box, the tail press can be considered a beginner trick. On a narrow rail, it's much more difficult to lock in and maintain balance.
